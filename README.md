# Global Energy Consumption Analysis

# Project Overview

Energy consumption plays a critical role in economic growth and environmental sustainability. This project analyzes global energy data to understand consumption patterns, renewable energy adoption, and carbon emissions across different countries.

# Objectives

Analyze global energy consumption trends.

Compare renewable vs fossil fuel usage.

Study country-wise energy patterns.

Identify factors affecting carbon emissions.

Provide insights for sustainable energy strategies.

# Technologies Used

SQL

PowerBI

# Data Cleaning & Preprocessing 

Checked for missing values and handled appropriately.

Removed duplicate records.

Converted data types where necessary.

Ensured data consistency across years and countries.

# Key Insights

USA (5.61M) and UK (5.44M) are the highest energy-consuming countries.

Energy consumption shows an overall increasing trend from 2000 to ~2020. Sharp drop around 2010 (~1.69M)

Fossil Fuel Dependency: 40.84% (highest). Industrial Use: 36.4%. Household Use: 22.76% (lowest).

Energy price fluctuates significantly over time. Lowest point around 2010 and Peak around 2013.

China (2597) and Australia (2580) have the highest emissions.

# Conclusion

The analysis shows that global energy consumption is steadily increasing, driven mainly by industrial activities and fossil fuel dependency. Countries with higher energy usage contribute significantly to carbon emissions, highlighting environmental concerns. Despite growth in energy demand, price fluctuations indicate market instability. This emphasizes the need for increased adoption of renewable energy sources to ensure sustainability and reduce environmental impact.
